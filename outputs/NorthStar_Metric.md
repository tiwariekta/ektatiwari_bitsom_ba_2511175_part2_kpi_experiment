# Task 2: North Star Metric Definition

## Selected North Star Metric

### Paid Conversion Rate

**Formula:**

```text
Paid Conversion Rate = Users Converted to Paid / Total Users
```

The Paid Conversion Rate has been selected as the North Star Metric for this experiment because the primary objective of the new onboarding campaign is to increase the number of users who become paying subscribers.

---

## Why This Is the Main Success Metric

The onboarding campaign is intended to guide new users through activation and ultimately encourage them to subscribe to a paid plan.

Paid Conversion Rate directly measures the percentage of users who successfully complete this journey and generate business value.

This metric is the most appropriate success measure because it:

* Directly aligns with the experiment objective.
* Represents a meaningful business outcome.
* Reflects customer acquisition effectiveness.
* Has a direct impact on revenue growth.

---

## Why Other Metrics Are Supporting Metrics

### Landing Page Visit Rate

Measures user exposure to the onboarding experience.

**Why it is not the North Star:**
A user visiting the landing page does not necessarily become a paying customer.

---

### Trial Start Rate

Measures user interest in trying the product.

**Why it is not the North Star:**
Users may start a trial but never convert into paying customers.

---

### Onboarding Completion Rate

Measures onboarding effectiveness.

**Why it is not the North Star:**
Completing onboarding does not guarantee subscription conversion.

---

### Engagement Score

Measures user activity and engagement.

**Why it is not the North Star:**
Highly engaged users may still remain free users and generate no revenue.

---

### Revenue per User

Measures financial performance.

**Why it is not the North Star:**
Revenue can be influenced by pricing plans, upgrades, and spending behavior. The experiment specifically focuses on improving user activation and conversion rather than pricing optimization.

---

## Connection to Business Growth

Paid Conversion Rate directly contributes to business growth through the following pathway:

```text
More Signups
      ↓
Higher Activation
      ↓
More Paid Conversions
      ↓
Higher Revenue
      ↓
Business Growth
```

Even a small improvement in conversion rate can result in a significant increase in recurring subscription revenue over time.

---

## Risks of Optimizing This Metric Blindly

Focusing only on conversion rate may create unintended consequences.

Potential risks include:

### Low-Quality Conversions

Users may convert initially but later request refunds.

### Increased Support Burden

Users may require additional support if onboarding becomes overly aggressive or confusing.

### Poor User Experience

Pressure-driven conversion tactics may negatively affect customer satisfaction.

### Revenue Quality Concerns

An increase in conversion does not always lead to higher long-term revenue.

### Segment-Level Impact

The treatment may improve overall conversion while negatively impacting specific user segments such as certain regions, devices, or traffic sources.

---

## Guardrail Metrics

To ensure that conversion improvements do not negatively affect business performance, the following guardrail metrics will also be monitored:

1. Refund Rate
2. Support Ticket Rate
3. Days to Convert
4. Engagement Score

These metrics help ensure that growth is sustainable and does not come at the expense of user experience or revenue quality.
